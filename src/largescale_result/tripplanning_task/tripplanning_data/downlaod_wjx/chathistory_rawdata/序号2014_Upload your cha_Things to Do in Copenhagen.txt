# Things to Do in Copenhagen

## Message From You:
I'm looking for things to do in Copenhagen

## Message From ChatGPT:
Sure, there are a lot of interesting spots in Copenhagen. Here are some recommendations:

1. **Amarienborg**: It's the home of the Danish royal family. You can witness the changing of the guard at 12:00. The admission price is $1.25 and it's open all week. It's just 10 minutes away from the Copenhagen airport.

2. **Frederiks Kirke**: Known for its modern architecture, it's a must-visit. The admission price is $20 and it's open from Tuesday to Friday.

3. **The Little Mermaid Statue**: This statue is a character from Grimm's Fairy Tales. It's free and open all week.

4. **Noma**: Enjoy a dinner at this world-famous restaurant. The price is around $100 and it's open all week.

5. **Rundetaarn**: It's a 17th-century astronomy tower. The admission price is DKK 40 and it's open all week.

6. **Christiansborg Palace**: A significant landmark both historically and politically. It's free and open from Tuesday to Sunday.

7. **National Museum of Denmark**: The country’s largest cultural history museum. The admission price is $10 and it's open from Tuesday to Sunday.

8. **Nyhavn**: Known for its colorful buildings, historic boats, and vibrant atmosphere. It's free and open all week.

9. **Tivoli Gardens**: Known for its enchanting atmosphere and thrilling amusement park rides. It's free and open from Monday to Saturday.

10. **Skagens Museum**: Showcases the works of the Skagen Painters. The admission price is 200 dkk, and it's open from Tuesday to Friday.

11. **LEGOLAND Billund Resort**: Offers a world of fun with attractions inspired by the iconic LEGO brick. The admission price is 299 dkk, and it opens all week.

Remember to plan your visit according to the opening days and hours of these places. Enjoy your trip to Copenhagen!
